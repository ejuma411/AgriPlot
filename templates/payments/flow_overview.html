📋 The Journey

6 Legal Steps from Commitment to Title Handover

The buyer should understand this flow before any payment begins.

01

Due Diligence Lock

Buyer pays the commitment fee, AgriPlot locks the plot for 7 days, and the official search plus survey or beacon pack is released for review.

02

Sale Agreement

The seller's lawyer drafts the agreement, both sides capture advocate details, and the 10% deposit arrangement is secured in the legal framework.

03

LCB Consent

For agricultural land, the Land Control Board consent and any spousal approvals must be uploaded before the process moves forward.

04

Valuation & Stamp Duty

The government valuation is recorded and the buyer uploads the KRA or eCitizen stamp duty receipt before the completion exchange can proceed.

05

Completion Exchange

The buyer clears the remaining balance to the legal side while the seller hands over the original title, signed transfer forms, and ID/KRA copies.

06

Registration & Handover

The lawyer lodges the transfer at the registry or Ardhisasa, and only a fresh registry result showing the buyer's name flips the plot to sold.

🔒 What stays blocked until evidence is uploaded

LCB consent step stays pending until the consent letter is uploaded.
Stamp duty step stays pending until valuation and the tax receipt are both captured.
Completion exchange stays pending until the title, RL1 forms, and seller identity pack are confirmed.
Registration is the only step that can flip the plot to sold.
